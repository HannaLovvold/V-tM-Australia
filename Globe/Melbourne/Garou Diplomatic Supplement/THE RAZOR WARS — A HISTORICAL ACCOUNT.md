# THE RAZOR WARS — A HISTORICAL ACCOUNT

*Compiled from Glass Walker oral tradition (Don Abacus's recorded 
testimony 1948-1973), Calabria family records (the Luisa Calabria 
chronicle), Camarilla Australian archives (the Adelaide Toreador 
Historical Society holdings), and miscellaneous primary sources 
including period mortal newspapers, where Razor Wars incidents 
were generally framed as bushfires, animal attacks, or 
unsolved disappearances.*

---

## ORIGINS (1880s-1907)

The conflict had been building for three decades before its formal 
outbreak. The original-six Kindred founders had arrived in Sydney 
in 1790 and had spent the early 19th century establishing 
feeding territories in the colonial settlement and its immediate 
hinterland; this expansion was relatively peaceful because Australian 
Garou populations were geographically distant from the small 
coastal Kindred settlements. From the 1850s onward, however, the 
gold rush and subsequent mortal-population explosion drove 
Kindred expansion into bushland and country regions where Garou 
septs operated under traditional caern-protection mandates.

By the 1880s, three pressure points had emerged. First, Brisbane 
and the Queensland goldfields produced extensive Kindred 
movement into the Glasshouse Mountains and the Sunshine Coast 
hinterland — bushland the Glass Walkers and other Garou tribes 
considered actively-defended Garou Zone. Second, Melbourne's 
post-1854 expansion drove Kindred presence into the 
Dandenong Ranges and the Werribee bushland, again Garou Zone. 
Third, Sydney's New South Wales hinterland expansion drove 
Kindred presence into the Blue Mountains and the South 
Coast bushland.

The first non-fatal incidents began in the 1880s — Garou 
intercept-and-warn operations against Kindred parties that 
had entered Garou Zones without protocol, with mortal mortality 
on both sides as a consequence of the warnings being misunderstood 
or escalated. The Kindred response was to reduce expedition 
sizes and operate more carefully; the Garou response was to 
formalise zone-marking through scent-trail and spirit-mediated 
boundary signals that the Kindred mostly could not perceive.

By 1900, Continental Sept Council records indicate that Australian 
Garou tribes had collectively decided that further Kindred 
expansion would require active Garou response. The Glass 
Walkers, as the urban-specialist tribe with the most Kindred-adjacent 
experience, took the leadership role in formulating that response. 
WALKER-OF-IRON-ROADS, the Glass Walker Athro who served as 
Continental Sept Council senior representative for southeastern 
Australia 1894-1907, drafted the response framework that the 
Council adopted in 1903.

The framework's three principles: (1) further Kindred entry 
into designated Garou Zones would be met with lethal force 
on first encounter rather than warning; (2) Kindred parties 
that had entered before notification would be permitted 
withdrawal-with-warning if they withdrew immediately; (3) 
Kindred infrastructure-building within designated Garou Zones 
(havens, herd-establishment, ghoul-network) would be destroyed 
without warning regardless of the Kindred party's intent.

The framework was not communicated to Kindred parties because 
no formal cross-supernatural diplomatic infrastructure existed 
to communicate through. Continental Sept Council records 
specifically note this as a known operational risk.

## OUTBREAK (1907)

**14 February 1907 — The Glasshouse Mountains Incident.**

A Brisbane Ventrue named CHARLES PRESTON-ASHCROFT (8th gen, 
Embraced 1854 in London, arrived in Brisbane 1881) led a 
hunting expedition of seven Kindred — three Ventrue, two 
Toreador, two Brujah — into the Glasshouse Mountains north of 
Brisbane on the night of 14 February 1907. The expedition's 
purpose was to investigate rumoured rare-blood-quality 
populations in the small mortal settlements of the Sunshine 
Coast hinterland; Preston-Ashcroft had heard from a 
Sydney-based contact that the area's mortals had a 
distinctive blood-Resonance that he wanted to examine.

The expedition entered the Glasshouse Mountains Garou Zone 
at approximately 11 PM. The Glass Walker pack monitoring 
that boundary, led by FOSTERN-RANK GAROU MARGARET 
'IRONHEART' COLBORNE, intercepted them at the Mount 
Tibrogargan approach trail at 11:23 PM.

The encounter went bad immediately. Preston-Ashcroft, who 
had not been informed about the new framework and who had 
expected at most a warning encounter, attempted to use 
Dominate on Ironheart Colborne to negotiate passage. The 
Dominate use registered to the Glass Walker pack as 
spiritual-violation and triggered Berserk Frenzy in two of 
the four pack members. The frenzy resulted in five Kindred 
Final Deaths within forty seconds (Preston-Ashcroft and 
two Toreador and two Brujah). Two Ventrue escaped — 
ALBERT WHITELAW (9th gen, escaped through Celerity 4) and 
HENRIETTA MARCH (10th gen, escaped because Ironheart 
Colborne specifically released her to carry warning back 
to Brisbane).

Whitelaw and March returned to Brisbane on 15 February 1907 
and reported the incident to the Brisbane Camarilla court 
under the Praxis of Prince GERALD AINSWORTH-HOWES (Ventrue, 
9th gen, Praxis since 1894). Ainsworth-Howes convened an 
emergency Council on 16 February 1907 and authorised retaliatory 
operations against Glass Walker septs in southeastern Queensland 
within 72 hours.

The retaliatory operations were conducted on the night of 
19 February 1907 by a Brisbane operations team of fifteen 
Kindred against the Glass Walker pack-house in the Conondale 
Range. The operations succeeded in destroying the pack-house 
and killing two of the four pack members (Ironheart Colborne 
herself and a Cliath-rank Garou named THOMAS RIVERMOUTH); 
the operations cost three Kindred lives and significant 
operational exposure (mortal witnesses had to be Dominated 
in nine separate locations, two of whom did not survive 
the Dominate-shock).

The Continental Sept Council convened emergency session on 
22 February 1907 and authorised continental-scale Garou 
mobilisation. The Razor Wars had begun.

## ESCALATION (1907-1914)

The first phase of the conflict ran from February 1907 through 
mid-1914 and was characterised by tit-for-tat operations 
across all three pressure points (Queensland, Victoria, 
New South Wales). Casualty pace: approximately 8-12 Kindred 
Final Deaths per year, approximately 3-5 Garou deaths per 
year, total roughly 70 Kindred and 25 Garou by mid-1914.

Notable incidents:

**June 1908 — The Werribee Massacre.** A Camarilla operations 
team attempting to clear a Garou-defended bushland site in 
Werribee was caught in a coordinated multi-pack ambush. Eleven 
Kindred Final Deaths in a single night, including the Sydney 
Camarilla Sheriff CLEMENTINE HAYWORTH (Ventrue, 9th gen). 
The Werribee Massacre was the largest single-incident 
Kindred loss of the war and produced significant Sydney 
Camarilla destabilisation.

**November 1909 — The Conondale Counter-Strike.** A combined 
Brisbane-Sydney Camarilla operation against a major Glass 
Walker caern in the Conondale Range. The operation succeeded 
in damaging the caern's spiritual binding (the caern was 
not destroyed but operated at diminished capacity for 
fourteen years) and killed five Garou including the 
Conondale Sept's Septleader. Camarilla casualties: nine 
Kindred Final Deaths.

**March 1911 — The Blue Mountains Incident.** A Sydney 
Camarilla expedition into the Blue Mountains was captured 
intact by a Glass Walker pack and held for negotiation. 
Sydney Camarilla refused negotiation under the Camarilla 
sect-political position that negotiation with Garou was 
categorically impossible. The expedition (six Kindred) was 
destroyed by the Glass Walker pack after seven days of 
captivity. The destruction's brutality produced Sydney 
Kindred political crisis that nearly toppled Sarrasine's 
domain (then in its early phase under his original Toreador 
cover).

**August 1913 — The Adelaide Stalemate.** Adelaide's Camarilla 
court under Prince Octavia Marsh's predecessor THEODORE 
MARSH (the same Marsh later ascended to torpor in 1923) 
attempted a different approach: formal cease-fire negotiation 
with the local Glass Walker sept. The negotiation reached 
preliminary agreement after three weeks. The agreement 
collapsed when continental Kindred-political pressure forced 
Theodore Marsh to repudiate it. The Adelaide Stalemate is 
historically significant as the first attempted formal 
diplomacy of the war and as the precedent for the eventual 
Cress Truce.

## CRISIS (1914-1920)

The second phase, 1914-1920, was characterised by escalating 
casualty pace and growing Kindred and Garou recognition that 
the conflict was unsustainable. By 1916, Garou Continental 
Sept Council records indicate that the Council was actively 
seeking diplomatic alternatives. By 1917, Camarilla 
Continental Conclave records indicate similar Kindred 
recognition.

The crisis was driven by three factors. First, the conflict's 
continued mortal-collateral cost was producing Masquerade 
violations at an accelerating rate; by 1917, Australian 
mortal newspapers carried "unexplained bushfire" and 
"feral animal attack" stories at a rate that would have 
been investigated by mortal authorities except for World 
War I's diversion of investigative resources. Second, Garou 
casualties were proving unreplaceable — the tribe's small 
population could not sustain continued losses at war-time 
pace. Third, Kindred casualties were producing political 
instability in every domain — the Brisbane court collapsed 
twice during this period, the Sydney court was destabilised 
under Sarrasine's shadowy continued operations, and 
Adelaide nearly fell to internal Camarilla coup.

Notable incidents:

**February 1915 — The Royal Park Incident.** A Camarilla 
operations team attacked the Royal Park caern in Melbourne. 
The attack succeeded in damaging the caern's spiritual binding 
similarly to the 1909 Conondale strike, but at a higher cost: 
fourteen Kindred Final Deaths in a single night, including 
significant senior Toreador casualties. The Royal Park 
Incident was the highest-casualty single Kindred operation 
of the war.

**September 1917 — The Bone Gnawer Diplomacy.** A Bone 
Gnawer pack in inner Melbourne attempted to broker an 
informal local cease-fire with Lytton's Camarilla Melbourne 
court. The negotiation reached preliminary agreement covering 
inner-Melbourne urban districts. The agreement was effective 
in those districts for fourteen months before continental 
escalation overrode it. The Bone Gnawer Diplomacy is 
historically significant as the second precedent (after the 
Adelaide Stalemate) for the eventual Cress Truce, and 
produced the lasting Glass-Walker-Bone-Gnawer diplomatic 
cooperation that continues into 2025.

**June 1919 — The Conondale Caern Restoration.** Despite 
ongoing conflict, the Glass Walker tribe successfully 
restored the Conondale caern to full operational capacity 
after the 1909 damage. The restoration required ten years 
of ritual work and was completed under continued Camarilla 
operational pressure. The successful restoration demonstrated 
to the Camarilla that the war was not winnable on Kindred 
operational terms — caerns could be damaged but not 
permanently destroyed, and the Garou would eventually 
restore everything the Kindred destroyed.

## RESOLUTION (1920-1924)

The third phase, 1920-1924, was the negotiation phase. 
Continental Sept Council and Continental Conclave both 
formally authorised diplomatic exploration in mid-1920. 
The exploration phase took eighteen months because no 
existing infrastructure for cross-supernatural diplomacy 
existed and both sides had to invent it.

The breakthrough came through the GIOVANNI FAMILY (now Hecata). 
The Giovanni's necromantic tradition produced a diplomatic 
register that both Kindred and Garou could read as legitimate 
— the family's death-respect tradition produced Vitae-resonance 
that Glass Walker Theurges identified as not-Wyrm-corruption, 
and the family's institutional standing as a non-Camarilla 
non-Sabbat independent gave them political flexibility that 
Camarilla negotiators lacked.

In late 1922, LUISA CALABRIA (Giovanni, 7th gen, Embraced 1837 
in Calabria, arrived Australia 1879 with the family's 
emigration, established the Australian family branch's 
matriarch position 1881) was approached jointly by the 
Continental Conclave and the Continental Sept Council to 
serve as cross-supernatural negotiator. She accepted in 
February 1923.

The Continental Sept Council's chosen counterpart was DON 
ABACUS (Glass Walker, Athro rank, Septleader of Royal Park 
Sept since 1903 — see PART 4 for full NPC build), who had 
been advocating for diplomatic resolution since 1916 and 
who was the Continental Sept Council's senior representative 
for southeastern Australia.

Negotiations began at the CRESS HOTEL on Bourke Street in 
Melbourne on 6 August 1923. The hotel was selected because 
it was: (1) located on Neutral Zone street under Glass Walker 
informal protocol, (2) operationally maintainable through 
Calabria family ghoul-network, (3) sufficient size for the 
delegations from approximately fourteen Kindred parties 
and approximately eleven Garou tribes, and (4) located 
near the Royal Park caern such that emergency Theurge 
ritual support was readily available.

Negotiations ran from 6 August 1923 through 14 February 1924 
— precisely seventeen years to the day after the Glasshouse 
Mountains Incident. The Truce was signed at 11:23 PM, 
matching the time of the original incident. The signing 
included formal Theurge Rite of Binding conducted by the 
Royal Park Sept's Master of the Rite (a Garou named 
ELDER-SISTER-OF-TURNED-EARTH who reached Final Death in 
1973) with Calabria family ritual support from Luisa 
herself. The Rite spirit-witnessed the agreement at 
sept-recognised level, which is the structural reason 
the Truce has held for 101 years.

The Razor Wars ended at 11:23 PM on 14 February 1924.

## AFTERMATH

Casualties: approximately 400 Kindred Final Deaths (representing 
roughly 30% of pre-war continental Kindred population), 
approximately 60-90 Garou deaths (representing roughly 25% of 
pre-war continental Garou population), and an estimated 600-1200 
mortal collateral fatalities (the upper estimate reflects 
suspected uncounted bushland deaths during the Conondale 
and Werribee operations).

Political consequences: the Camarilla's Australian operations 
required twenty years to rebuild to pre-war institutional 
capacity. The Anarch Movement's Australian wing emerged 
during the rebuilding period as Anarchs proved better 
suited to post-Razor-Wars political conditions than the 
Tower's pre-war structure. The Glass Walker Australian 
septs took thirty years to rebuild Garou population to 
pre-war levels and never fully restored the Continental 
Sept Council's pre-1907 operational capacity. The Bone 
Gnawer and Children of Gaia presence in Australia grew 
during the rebuilding period as the Glass Walkers 
delegated diplomatic-cooperation roles to allied tribes.

Cultural consequences: every modern Australian Kindred 
or Garou under approximately seventy years of unlife 
or age has had Razor Wars history communicated to them as 
foundational training. The phrase "we are not going to 
have another Razor Wars" appears in approximately fifty 
percent of all Australian cross-supernatural diplomatic 
correspondence. The 14 February anniversary is observed 
quietly each year by senior Kindred and Garou through 
small individual rituals; Eliza Hawkins-Greene visits the 
former Cress Hotel site each 14 February, as did Don 
Abacus and Marcus Cobalt before her.

The Razor Wars are not history in the Kindred-Garou 
sense. They are recent. They are the boundary that 
Australian supernatural politics will not cross again 
unless catastrophe forces the crossing.
