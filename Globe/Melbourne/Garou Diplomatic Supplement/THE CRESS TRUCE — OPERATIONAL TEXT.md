# THE CRESS TRUCE — OPERATIONAL TEXT

*This document presents the Cress Truce in playable form — the 
articles cited in scenes, the protocols that govern emergency 
response, and the procedures the quarterly Truce Council follows. 
The original 1924 treaty is approximately seventy pages of dense 
cross-supernatural law; this is the operational summary used 
by working diplomatic operatives in 2025.*

---

## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Treaty:

- **Kindred** means undead beings derived from Caine through Embrace, 
  including all clans recognised by Camarilla, Anarch Movement, 
  Sabbat, Hecata Family Council, and Independent operations.
- **Garou** means shapeshifting children of Gaia, including all 
  thirteen tribes operating in Australia.
- **Kinfolk** means mortals related to Garou by blood or marriage 
  who carry Garou heritage without First Change.
- **Ghoul** means mortals supernaturally bound to Kindred through 
  Vitae-feeding.
- **Mortal** means humans not otherwise classified above.
- **Sept** means a Garou social-religious organisation centered on 
  a caern.
- **Caern** means a Garou sacred location where the Gauntlet 
  is thin.
- **Coterie** means a Kindred social unit of three to six members.
- **Kindred Zone** means territory designated under Article 4 
  for primary Kindred operational use.
- **Garou Zone** means territory designated under Article 4 
  for primary Garou operational use.
- **Neutral Zone** means territory not designated as Kindred 
  Zone or Garou Zone, governed by case-by-case protocols.

---

## ARTICLE 2 — FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

The Parties to this Treaty acknowledge:

(a) Both parties have legitimate operational interest in 
    Australian territory.
(b) Sustained conflict produces mutual catastrophic loss as 
    demonstrated by the Razor Wars.
(c) Coexistence requires clear territorial designation, 
    formal incident response protocols, and continuing 
    diplomatic infrastructure.
(d) Each party retains sovereignty within its designated 
    operational zones subject to mutual protections under 
    this Treaty.
(e) Neither party concedes ideological or theological position; 
    this Treaty is operational coexistence, not reconciliation.

---

## ARTICLE 3 — MORTAL PROTECTION

(a) Both parties commit to maintaining the **Masquerade**, the 
    Garou Veil, and equivalent supernatural-concealment 
    protocols affecting their respective populations.
(b) Mortals who become aware of supernatural existence through 
    one party's operational error become joint protection 
    responsibility — the discovering party notifies the other 
    within 24 hours, and joint response is coordinated 
    through the appropriate Truce Council channel.
(c) **Kinfolk are protected from Kindred feeding** under all 
    circumstances. Feeding from kinfolk is automatic Truce 
    breach. Kinfolk identification protocols are maintained 
    by Garou septs and shared with Kindred Sheriffs through 
    quarterly intelligence exchange.
(d) **Children (mortal humans under 16 years of age)** are 
    protected from supernatural action by either party in 
    all circumstances. This protection is absolute.
(e) Mortal collateral damage during legitimate operations is 
    minimised; the responsible party investigates incidents 
    and reports findings to the Truce Council.

## ARTICLE 4 — TERRITORIAL DESIGNATION

Australia is divided into three zone categories, with detailed 
maps maintained by the Truce Council and updated by amendment 
at quarterly meetings:

### **KINDRED ZONES**

Designated for primary Kindred operational use. Garou may enter 
under Article 7 protocols. Kindred Zones include:

- **Melbourne CBD** (within standard boundaries: Flinders Street, 
  Spring Street, La Trobe Street, Spencer Street)
- **Carlton, Fitzroy, Collingwood, North Melbourne, Brunswick** 
  (with specific exclusions for Royal Park and Princes Park)
- **St Kilda and bayside districts** (north of Sandringham)
- **South Yarra, Toorak, Prahran, Windsor, and the inner southeast**
- **Footscray and the western suburbs** (Anarch-administered 
  Kindred Zone with Red Mag's Baronage holding operational 
  authority)
- **Sydney CBD and inner districts** (Sarrasine-domain Kindred 
  Zone, separately governed; the Truce applies in modified form 
  in Sydney by Sarrasine-specific 1888 amendment)
- **Adelaide CBD and inner districts**
- **Equivalent zones in Brisbane** (note: Brisbane is currently 
  Sabbat-controlled and the Truce does not apply; Glass Walker 
  septs do not enter Brisbane and Sabbat operations are 
  excluded from cooperative protection)
- **Hobart inner districts** (Tasmania, smaller-scale designation)
- **Perth CBD and inner districts** (small designation, no 
  formal Camarilla Praxis)

### **GAROU ZONES**

Designated for primary Garou operational use. Kindred may 
enter under Article 7 protocols. Garou Zones include:

- **Royal Park, Princes Park, Carlton North parks** (Melbourne)
- **The Dandenong Ranges** (substantial wilderness Garou Zone 
  east of Melbourne, running from Mount Dandenong through 
  the Yarra Valley)
- **Werribee River corridor and bushland** (western Melbourne)
- **Kinglake region** (north of Melbourne)
- **You Yangs Regional Park** (southwest of Melbourne)
- **Mornington Peninsula** (south of Sandringham, full Garou 
  Zone)
- **Blue Mountains** (NSW)
- **Glasshouse Mountains and Sunshine Coast hinterland** 
  (Queensland; symbolic significance as Razor Wars origin)
- **Conondale Range** (Queensland)
- **Substantial regional bushland across NSW, Victoria, 
  Tasmania, and WA** as detailed in maps
- **Tasmanian wilderness** (most of southwestern Tasmania)

### **NEUTRAL ZONES**

The remainder. Governed by case-by-case protocols including:

- **Albert Park Lake** (Melbourne)
- **The Yarra River corridor itself**
- **Port Phillip Bay** (formally neutral but Garou-favoured)
- **Most regional and rural Australia not in designated zones**
- **Canberra** (special diplomatic ground, neither party 
  conducts territorial operations)

Zone designation is formally reviewed at the centennial Truce 
Council (every 100 years) and amended at the quarterly Truce 
Council as needed for emerging issues.

## ARTICLE 5 — OPERATIONAL RIGHTS WITHIN ZONES

### **Within Kindred Zones:**

(a) Kindred conduct operations under sect-internal protocols 
    without Garou interference.
(b) Garou may enter for diplomatic purposes (Article 7), 
    investigation of suspected Wyrm-corruption (Article 8), 
    or Truce-enforcement matters (Article 9), each requiring 
    appropriate notification.
(c) Kindred Zones include **kinfolk no-feeding zones** at 
    locations where significant kinfolk populations work 
    or live (universities, specific medical facilities, 
    police stations, designated community centres).

### **Within Garou Zones:**

(a) Garou conduct operations under sept-internal protocols 
    without Kindred interference.
(b) Kindred may enter through **Article 7 notification 
    protocol**: contacting the relevant Glass Walker sept's 
    designated emergency-protocol channel at least 4 hours 
    in advance, providing identification, purpose, and duration 
    of intended presence.
(c) **Unsanctioned Kindred presence in Garou Zones triggers 
    Article 9 emergency response.**

### **Within Neutral Zones:**

(a) Both parties operate under mutual-respect protocols.
(b) No party may conduct operations that exclude the other 
    from Neutral Zone access.
(c) Disputes are resolved through Truce Council quarterly 
    review.

## ARTICLE 6 — KINDRED-INTERNAL FACTIONAL ARRANGEMENTS

The Truce recognises the following Kindred factional 
distinctions for operational purposes:

(a) **Camarilla operations** in designated Camarilla domains 
    (Melbourne, Adelaide, parts of Hobart) are governed by 
    Princely authority that holds Truce-counterpart status.
(b) **Anarch operations** in Anarch territories (Footscray, 
    Brunswick, parts of Sydney's western fringe) are governed 
    by Baronial authority that holds Truce-counterpart status.
(c) **Hecata Family operations** are governed by family 
    matriarchy with separate Truce-counterpart status (the 
    family was party to the original 1924 negotiation through 
    Luisa Calabria).
(d) **Sarrasine's Sydney domain** holds independent 
    Truce-counterpart status under modified 1888 protocols.
(e) **Sabbat operations** are excluded from Truce protection 
    and do not hold Truce-counterpart status anywhere on 
    the continent.

The Truce does not adjudicate intra-Kindred sect disputes; 
those are managed by Kindred-internal Lake-Treaty-equivalent 
infrastructure.

## ARTICLE 7 — DIPLOMATIC PROTOCOLS

(a) **Quarterly Truce Council** meets four times per year at 
    the Melbourne Town Hall (or other agreed neutral location 
    for non-Melbourne agenda items). Standing attendees 
    include: the senior Glass Walker Septleader for southeastern 
    Australia (Eliza Hawkins-Greene), the Camarilla Sheriff 
    (Sister Fatima Salih-El) representing Camarilla Australia, 
    the Anarch Council senior representative (rotated; currently 
    Red Mag in 2025), the Hecata family senior negotiator 
    (Sofia Calabria-Hecata), and observers from Children of 
    Gaia (continental peacemaker tribe representative) and 
    Bone Gnawer (continental urban tribe representative).
(b) **Bilateral protocols** for non-Council matters: each 
    Truce-counterpart maintains designated emergency contact 
    channels accessible 24/7 with response time of 4 hours 
    or less.
(c) **Cross-supernatural meetings** at neutral or non-zone 
    locations require homid-form-only attendance by Garou 
    parties and Discipline-baseline-only attendance by 
    Kindred parties.
(d) **Spirit witness** is acknowledged at all formal Truce 
    proceedings; Glass Walker Theurges may invoke spirit 
    presence and Kindred parties acknowledge the invocation 
    formally.

## ARTICLE 8 — JOINT THREAT RESPONSE

The Parties commit to joint response to threats affecting 
both parties:

(a) **Sabbat operations** in any non-Brisbane Australian 
    location trigger joint response. Response coordination 
    occurs through Truce Council emergency channels with 
    operational planning shared between the relevant Kindred 
    domain authority and the relevant Glass Walker sept.
(b) **Twilight Project / Second Inquisition operations** 
    affecting either party trigger joint intelligence-sharing 
    and, where operationally feasible, joint response. The 
    AFP-Special-Operations partnership with Twilight Project 
    is treated as joint threat for this purpose.
(c) **Pentex operations** are Garou-primary response with 
    Kindred intelligence support; Kindred-led counter-Pentex 
    operations are not standard but have occurred (the 1987 
    Geelong Pentex Subsidiary Incident is the precedent).
(d) **Wyrm-corruption events** affecting designated Garou 
    Zones are Garou-primary response with no Kindred operational 
    role; Kindred presence during such events is by invitation 
    only.
(e) **Mass Masquerade-violation events** trigger joint response 
    with operational lead determined by event type.

## ARTICLE 9 — INCIDENT RESPONSE — THE 72-HOUR PROTOCOL

This is the protocol cited in Scenario 15 (the Sofia/Cress Council 
scenario) and the operational core of the Truce's day-to-day 
functioning.

### **The Trigger**

Any of the following events triggers the 72-Hour Protocol:

- **Kindred-on-kinfolk physical contact in a Garou Zone**
- **Kindred entry into Garou Zone without Article 7 notification**
- **Garou-on-mortal-non-kinfolk physical contact in a Kindred Zone**
- **Garou entry into Kindred Zone without diplomatic purpose**
- **Cross-zone mortal-collateral damage attributable to either 
  party**
- **Discovery of Wyrm-corruption infrastructure within a 
  Kindred Zone**

### **Hour 0 — Notification**

The detecting party (typically the Glass Walker sept's 
emergency-protocol channel for Garou-detected incidents, or 
the relevant Kindred Sheriff for Kindred-detected incidents) 
formally notifies the other party within 4 hours of detection.

### **Hours 0-24 — Initial Response**

Both parties conduct independent initial investigation within 
24 hours. The Truce Council convening is scheduled if not 
already in session.

### **Hours 24-48 — Joint Council**

The four Truce-counterparts (Camarilla Sheriff, Anarch 
representative, Hecata family negotiator, Septleader) plus 
the host (typically Sofia Calabria-Hecata at Calabria Family 
Funerals or the Melbourne Town Hall depending on incident 
profile) convene formal Cress Council. The Council:

(a) Hears testimony from involved parties.
(b) Reviews evidence gathered during Hours 0-24.
(c) Receives Truce-counterpart positions on resolution 
    parameters.
(d) Begins formal negotiation on resolution.

Council sessions can be 4-12 hours long. Multiple sessions 
across the 24-72 hour window are typical.

### **Hours 48-72 — Resolution**

Resolution must be reached by Hour 72. Acceptable resolutions 
include:

- **Perpetrator punishment** by the perpetrator's faction 
  with cross-faction witness (the May 2025 Robbo Carrozza 
  resolution: Final Death by Red Mag personally with joint 
  witnesses).
- **Reparations** (Vitae compensation to affected kinfolk for 
  medical care; financial compensation through ghoul-network; 
  ritual compensation through Theurge ceremony).
- **Structural prevention measures** (the Brunswick enclave 
  formalisation under Footscray Baronage, post-May 2025).
- **Joint statement of incident closure** with signed 
  acknowledgment by all four Truce-counterparts.

### **Failure Mode — Continental Cress Council Escalation**

If resolution is not reached by Hour 72, the matter escalates 
to **Continental Cress Council** — a six-week formal proceeding 
involving Glass Walker septs from across Australia, Camarilla 
domain representatives from all Australian Camarilla cities, 
Hecata family branch representatives, Anarch Council 
representatives, and observer-status delegates from other 
Garou tribes operating in Australia. The Continental Cress 
Council has not been used since 1962 and the parties consider 
its use a structural failure of the bilateral mechanism. 
Avoiding Continental escalation is itself part of why the 
72-hour deadline is enforced.

---

## ARTICLE 10 — AMENDMENT

The Treaty may be amended through:

(a) **Quarterly Truce Council amendment** for minor procedural 
    revisions, requiring unanimous Truce-counterpart agreement.
(b) **Centennial renewal** every 100 years (most recent: 2024) 
    for substantive revisions, conducted as formal multi-month 
    negotiation by senior Kindred and Garou negotiators.
(c) **Emergency amendment** in response to chronicle-defining 
    events (e.g., the 1976 Brisbane Sabbat fall, which produced 
    the modified Brisbane-exclusion clauses still in effect).

---

## ARTICLE 11 — TERMINATION

The Treaty terminates only through:

(a) **Mutual formal renunciation** by both parties at the 
    Truce-counterpart level.
(b) **Catastrophic breach** by one party that cannot be 
    remedied through Article 9 protocols, requiring the other 
    party's formal renunciation.

The Treaty has not been formally terminated and the Parties 
do not anticipate termination. Even during the 1976 Brisbane 
crisis, the Parties maintained the Treaty for the rest of 
the continent and modified rather than terminated.
